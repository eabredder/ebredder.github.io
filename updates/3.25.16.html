Materials & Processing

#Making it Happen
Enjoyed another productive week full of projects and new materials! It was great to get a few students started on the arc welder for a week of practice. Timothy's bat is coming along well, mallets are being completed on the lathe, experiments with melting rubber for fishing lures, Emmett's work on new metal-working bench, great laser cut signs for the shop from Skyler, and unique project designs are coming along like Robert's planter. This has been an exciting week of material discovery and process development. Looking forward to seeing what occurs this coming week!

Computer Science


The Joysticks of Learning
Developing our projects and computer science curriculum. We have spent the last week creating plans and schedules around our ideas so that we can extend them outside of our classroom. We are looking to share our ideas online in an open source community, build out development revisions, or begin entreprenuerial ventures. Some great CAD applications and practice from Alex, beginning Java development with Elijah, Vasil, and Lee, sprite pack creation from Mitchell, server upgrades with Caleb and Mats, and lots of skills development in many languages. Keep watching for some amazing project developments and their presence online as we continue to work towards creating great products and software.

I Saw the Sign
Another week of developing skills in C and building out bigger project goals for Computer Science 2 guys. We also spent some time working on physical signs for the drama production, Memphis. Jacob has built the frames, Zach is creating the font and design to be laser cut and we are developing the firmware to control the signs, one to 5 feet wide (pictures to come). Continued success with SonicPi and fun practical skill with a few chess matches. Great progress and a lot of excitement all around.
