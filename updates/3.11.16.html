MHS - Bredder - Updates: 3.11.16
Materials & Processing

Brush your shoulder off.
Finishing up some really great mallets. We have put some good hours into the lathe and prepping our work using the jointer, planer, miter saw and squaring things up with the table saw. Learning the importance of square material, tight glue-ups, making sure things are accurately measured and double-checked. Shout-out to Ben for going the extra mile in recreating his mallet head to make sure it is perfect. Timmy, Emmett, and Trevor worked well on applying a nice finish to their mallets of all different woods. Looking forward to our next project and continuing to develop our shed plans for the tennis team!

Computer Science


Game, boy.
Another wild week in Computer Science I with animations, 3D printing and CAD design, more language development mixed with logic, and a lot of unique projects being developed. Students have been designing their own games, websites, robots, animations, hacks, and more. Thanks to Lee for sharing his work on OpenSCAD to the whole class and Josiah and Kyle for continuing to work through a wealth of difficult obstacles and sharing them all with the rest of us. The handheld video game system is awesome. Looking forward to another strong week of design challenges and deeper learning in computer science. Thanks all for another great week!

Continued..
Taking apart a 3D printer to make sure we can use flexible filament for our 3D designs from OpenSCAD. Okay (David/Clay). Creating animations of pixel-based art for our own game ideas. Yes (Trevor). Making music with Ruby scripts and recreating popular songs. Obviously (Michael). Taking on story writing and developing complex characters and connections. Not a problem (Zach). Building furniture while learning C and Batch file creation. Multitask this (Jacob). Need we say more?
