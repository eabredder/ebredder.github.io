Materials & Processing

We're going in!
Some great unique projects and shop help over the past few days! The table saw outfeed table is super sturdy and level. Thanks Emmett and Timmy, and thanks for teaching each other a few tricks. Trevor has been successful at making original turkey calls out of cherry and oak. He has learned from his first iteration and efficiently modified the newest version! Robert’s lathe project is a great success as seen from above. He is working hard and precisely - a master at the lathe. Willy has had some success with the ultimate gaming and entertainment chair by modifying an actual car seat to his needs. Excited to see the outcome with working speakers. Skyler continues to design beautiful artistic pieces on the laser cutter. Logan has successfully managed to weld his sprocket onto a weed eater engine. Enough said. More projects and excitement as the year comes close to an end!

Computer Science


Four Score and...
This has been an exciting few days watching all of the semester long projects take shape. Zach shared his development with moving enemies in Unity3D/C# that will eventually fire back! Reed shared his baseball bat software that will allow anyone to design a virtual bat with up to 20 or so different cross sections for ultimate customization. William shared his newest 4 person additions to his flash game and has shared everything up on Github here. Ricky shared his success with GameMaker and understanding the importance of making sure scripts are written for the right objects! Although when your object blows up into a million pieces that don’t go away - it looks cool. Alex shared his use with the new FormLabs Form2 3D resin printer and creating his 5th iteration of joysticks in Autodesk Fusion360. He has become a master of all 3D printers. Team #Java comprised of Elijah, Lee, and Vasil shared their work with developing calculators and conversion programs and the importance of a fluid GUI. This is not even half of the amazing stuff ready to get going for our Spring “Show ‘N Tell” coming in 2 weeks! Students will be sharing their work, progress, and ideas in multiple styles!

Teardown
The Cale Elementary School Minecraft/3D print project is coming to a close. The school is successfully drafted and will be handed off to the elementary school students to finish up designs and print! Check out the play this weekend and all the hard work Jake has put into the beautiful light up signs! The class was super helpful in designing and building these works of technological art! Lastly, Michael has successfully torn apart 4 computers and is putting them back together! We have begun a few rounds of the E-Cycling world. Check out the progress that David has made on the website: Monticello E-Cycling. Trevor and Zach have been actively preparing the Java-based program. This has been quite a journey moving into object-oriented programming from our start in C language. They have successfully compiled and run a few basic programs and will begin to devise a binary tree for the events in the E-Cycling industry and how to manage some products that come in! Clay is steadfast at keeping the group together and managing the workload, while designing custom parts in OpenSCAD. Great last few days!
