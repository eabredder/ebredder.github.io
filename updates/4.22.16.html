MoHS - Bredder - Updates: 4.22.16
Materials & Processing

#teamwork
Incredible week with Ms. Soto's ESOL class designing and building a tennis storage shed for atheltics. We spent the week getting to know each other and designing/building the shed. Big shout out to Timmy, Logan, and Emmett for leading 3 teams that framed the 2 sides and base. Props to Willy for really laying the groundwork. Skyler has been working hard at filming all of the progress. Ben has developed a daily blog in English and Spanish that describes the work we are doing! Check that out at the Mustang's Shed Blog. Overall an incredible amount of sharing, learning, working, building, and designing in one community-based project. Looking forward to finishing next week. Come join us on Friday, April 29, 9:30-10:30 for the unveiling of the project!

Computer Science


Array of Sunshine
Solid week creating some amazing projects. Reed just killing it with some bat designs that will coincide with his design software. Jonah created a trophy for the poetry slam coming up, that Connor is building a website for from Javascript, HTML, and CSS. Mitchel has developed more complicated pixel-based sprites to share online, while Henry develops games that are too complicated for his own good. Games inside games. Great shares this week from Mckenzie and her music-based website that combines all types of music so the user can compare various genres and Ricky for creating a game in GameMaker using GML scripting that destroys asteroids. Playing off some classics. Looking forward to LCD screen being finished soldering on the GameBoy/RaspberryPi/Awesomeness (Josiah/Kyle) and some custom joysticks in CAD (Alex). Zach is creating a 3D environment in Unity3D that is blowing my mind. So much more...

Check, One, Two - We're Live.
Big week for the new CS2 project: E-cycling. Check out their website at www.monticelloe-cycling.com thanks to David. Michael has perservered on the first major project and soldered/replaced some capacitors on an old motherboard. We will be retesting next week to see how things are working. Clay has developed some great ideas around everything and is focusing on UX/UI for some efficiency software that Zach and Trevor are hammering away at. We hope to look at forms of repurposing, fixing, and sharing experience through the website and software created in order to help other E-cycling ventures. Jacob has been helping with signs for the Memphis play at school and will be programming an LED grid next week for custom sign designs! The ON AIR signs are incredible. Lots of great progress this week in teamwork, design roles, and understanding hardware on a deeper level.
