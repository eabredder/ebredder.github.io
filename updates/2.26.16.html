Materials & Processing

Spin 'em right round.
Incredible progress this week! Everyone is set up on the lathes, we have helped each other get ready, shared skills, and designed our own mallets to keep and build our own tool collections. This is just awesome. It has been busy and productive. From design to making sure the stock is ready, we have been producing some beautiful pieces. For some, this is the first time on the lathe and everyone has been incredibly gentle and precise. Many different designs, some long and others thick. What a pleasure to be a part of all this!

Computer Science


Scrolling, Scrollin, Scrollin, Scrollin, C'Mon
Another fantastic week with a variety of projects, learning, and building that keeps expanding. As we keep progressing into scripting langauges and developing our own games and applications, we have touched upon over 10 different langauges being used in the same class period. Shoutouts: Alex gave us a great introduction to Ruby on Rails and talked about what skills he is developing. William has developed an intricate pong style game using Actionscript 3.0 with multiple levels and advances in difficulty (test an early version here). Josiah and Kyle are building and programming a RaspberryPi based MAME arcade in the style of a GameBoy. Cool. Off to the races...
Fun week in Computer Science II with some team building activities and continuing onward with our game designs, learning arrays in C, playing with Arduino, taking apart game controllers, making more pixel art, reviewing batch files, and more! We have started reading The Mythical Man Month by Frederick Brooks for some conversation pieces and a software programmer's life lessons in 1975. Michael has started Ruby with SonicPi and is creating songs through programming!
