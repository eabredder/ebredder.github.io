Materials & Processing

reLAXin
Taking everything a step further! Everyone has gotten a taste of the woodworking machines and tools, we have spent some time doing smaller projects, and the excitement has begun. We have been working on our own ideas this week, while finishing up some of the skill-based projects. Shoutout to Trevor, Jadiss, Tim, and Emmett for developing their own cart that fits specific dimensions for the lacrosse water jugs. No more carrying large jugs of water to the field! Great design practice, amazing teamwork, real problems and applications, and just a great product done in a few days. Thanks to Tim for continuing to help design products for our shop like a crosscut sled for the table saw. Skyler continues to rock the laser cutter to make some awesome and unique signs for the shop area!

Computer Science


Servers and more
As we have begun to plan out our curricular desings, we have been looking into developing games, consoles for these games, 3D printing and CAD, animations, website ideas utilizing facets of JavaScript, and creating hardware for remote controlled vehicles. Looks like we have our work cut out for us. We spent the week coming up with a plan of action, calendar/timeline, looking at resources and needs, while developing a deeper understanding of what we are trying to accomplish. We hope to continue to develop a plan that allows for a greater purpose in our learning like sharing our plans, code, and ideas, building projects that could lead to marketable products, and involving ourselves in the greater online community. Thanks to Jonah for sharing his prototyping skills and idea creation utilizing Scratch-based games, Reid for sharing his work in animations using Flash, and Mats and Caleb for developing an Agar.io, Linux-based server for everyone in the class to collaborate and enjoy while understanding how computers and servers interact. Lots of fun all around!

If there is a will there is a way!
More continuation on great ideas and developing skills. We have spent a little time on the hardware piece and working through troubleshooting a variety of issues that have come up as a result of our curiousity and creativity, like making new parts for a 3D Printer to print flexible parts to make custom gamepad buttons for a controller using OpenSCAD. Whew. Excited to see some of the developing ideas especially the in depth story Zach is working on for his game outline. Michael's perserverence in creating music in SonicPi has produced amazing results and MUSIC! Tons of great animations from Trevor provide unique art and excitement to our coding possibilities. Game design has taken on new levels and everyone is beginning to find their niche in creating parts of the whole. Much more excitement to come!
