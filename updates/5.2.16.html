Materials & Processing

Que Bueno
What an amazing two weeks building and collaborating with Ms. Soto’s ESOL class! Check out the full scoop on the combined blog by our very own Ben. The preview video shows the first week's progress thanks to Mr. Glover's students and footage from Skyler, Trevor, and Jadiss! Big shout out to Timmy, Emmett, and Robert for continuing to share experience and skills with our Spanish-speaking guests. They worked non-stop for 2 weeks straight! Logan did an amazing job teaching students how to properly use the tools and do some final touches on the shed. Willy did an incredible job painting literally everything. It looks great and he even fought with the rainy weather! I am so impressed with this class and am looking forward to a couple more great weeks. The project was so exciting Pam Moran, our superintendent came to check it out with the former Secretary of Education, Laura Fornash in the rain! This shed has a few little tweaks and it will be ready for the summer so the Tennis Team can take full usage of it. Great job sharing, working, collaborating, learning, and managing a tough project in just 8 class periods with completely new students. You guys rock. Computer Science


Life of Computer Science
Next level programming this week. We are pushing to put everything together for the last month of school! Here some actual next levels authored by William and Henry while Mitchel figures out how to animate his characters using Photoshop. Ricky is pushing through some accidental artistic explosions in GameMaker while Trevor continues to amaze the world with detailed pixel art and game design. Damian, Brayan, Ryan, and Jeremy made some amazing progress getting their motors to work with the old Lego bricks. Looking forward to some CAD design for the bases of the RC cars. The beginning of Reid’s baseball bat design software is looking promising! Zach’s first person action game in Unity has come a long way and is looking amazing. The gameboy project with Josiah and Kyle has made a ton of progress while matching tons of GPIO pins and a lot of soldering. Mckenzie’s HTML, CSS, and Javascript based website is taking shape! What perseverance. Excited to see more progress from the server side on Caleb and Mats' attacking node.js. Elijah, Vasil, and Lee have taken away with creating a super-modular calculator/convertor in Java. They have come a long way! Jonah really set up the Poetry Slam this year by creating laser cut signs with Adan and the amazing trophy! Also shoutout to Connor for making the website for their club, here! Lastly, Alex was a major help with Mr. Hickey's class, single-handedly fixing 3D printers and helping math students with CAD so that they could design their own ideas. This is how happy those kids are. Whew what a week!

Mine!
While continuing on an amazing journey into E-cycling business, we also managed to foster learning for Cale Elementary students. Clay helped layout the groundwork for our binary tree in solving the E-cycling madness and developing a true starting point for our co-lead programmers, Zach and Trevor. They have been hammering away at the keys learning Java to create a software that makes this entire industry easy to use, hence they are trying to save the world and salvage tech. David continues to impress us with his work on the website while Michael tears through a desktop computer to fix leaky capacitors and bent processor pins. Great team structure designed by the students. Jake is a killer sign maker and has helped the drama students’ play (Buy tickets here for next week’s showings) out with more signs; this week an LED-based programmable sign that he is soldering strips together in particular order. On top of all of this, they managed to set up a level in Minecraft to build the entire Cale school in less than 2 days with Mr. Jacoby. They then went to Cale with Mrs. Kessler to teach and build with the kids in hopes to 3D print an entire model for Cale's 25th Anniversary Celebration in May! More to come on this as the year finishes up...
